# B2B SaaS company - Selling tiered software subscriptions 
(Starter/Pro/Enterprise)

# Product - Subscription software: Three tiers, cheap to expensive
Classic SaaS (Software as a Service) ladder - small plan for small company/individual, big plan for big company.

Starter Plan, Pro Plan, Enterprise Plan
Priced at: $29, $99, $299

# Customers Table - Segment column
SMB, Enterprise, Mid-Market - these are business-size labels, not people-size labels
So, customers are companies buying software seats, not individual shoppers.

# How they sell it - Funnel-based sales, not shopping-cart style
Path: Visit website -> lead -> opportunity -> order
Classic B2B sales motion

# How they find customers - Four Channels
Paid Search - Customer clicked a paid ad on a search engine (Google Ads, Bing Ads),
Email - Customer came from a marketing email / newsletter link,
Organic - Customer found you via unpaid search engine results (SEO),
Social - Customer came from a social media post or ad (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, etc).
